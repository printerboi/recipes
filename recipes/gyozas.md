# Gyoza

![](/recipes/images/gyoza.jpeg)

## Ingredients

### For 3 servings

* Gyoza dough sheets (either fresh or frozen)
* 1/4 of a cabbage
* 500 g minced meat
* 3 spring onions
* 2 cloves of garlic
* 1 large spoon of ginger
* 2 large spoons of soy sauce
* 5 large spoons of sesame oil
* A bowl of water

## Cooking

### Preparations

* Either prepare the fresh dough or defrost the sheets at least an hour before cooking.

### Preparing the Filling

* Add the minced meat to a large bowl.
* Cut the onions into fine slices and add them to the bowl.
* Either press or finely chop the garlic and add it to the meat–onion mixture.
* Wash the cabbage and quarter it. Slice the cabbage as finely as possible and add it to the bowl.
* Peel the ginger and grate it into the mixture.
* Add the soy sauce and sesame oil to the meat mixture.
* Mix everything together thoroughly with your hands.

### Making the Gyoza

* After the dough sheets have been defrosted, dry the packaging to make sure the dough does not become wet.
* Unwrap the dough and place it on a clean towel.
* Fill a small bowl with water and put it next to the dough.
* Prepare a baking tray with baking paper and place it near your working area. We will use the tray to store the prepared gyoza.
* Take one sheet of dough and place it in your hand.
* Take 1/2 of a large spoon of the meat mixture and put it into the center of the dough sheet.
* With the other hand, wet your index finger and rub it over the lower half of the dough sheet.
* Move the upper half toward the lower half and cover the filling.
* Press both halves together so the gyoza is closed.
* To finish the gyoza, place the rim of the dumpling between your index finger and thumb at one side. While keeping your thumb stationary, move the index finger to the left to create a small fold.
* Repeat this along the whole rim of the gyoza. Make sure the dumpling stays closed.

### Baking them in the Airfryer

Gyozas can be prepared in several ways. A fast way of preparing them is to place them in the air fryer. Make sure your air fryer has a second level so we can add water that does not touch the gyoza.

* Add a little bit of water (around 50 ml, depending on your air fryer).
* Place the gyoza in the air fryer. Make sure they do not touch each other.
* Bake the gyoza for about 10–15 minutes at 180 °C.

The water should evaporate so we achieve a bit of a "steaming" effect. Another approach would be to brush the gyoza with a little bit of water before baking them. This should achieve a similar effect.

### Vegetarian/Vegan Option

If you are looking for a vegetarian or vegan option, replace the normal cabbage with kimchi and use a block of regular tofu as a protein-based replacement.
